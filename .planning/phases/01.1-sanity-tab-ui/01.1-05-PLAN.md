**Locked design choices for this plan (call out so executor doesn't re-litigate):**

1. **barW = 18 (was 14).** 18px column reads better than 14px at the right-edge orientation introduced in Plan 04. Justification: visually closer to the user's reference image gauge thickness; still fits comfortably alongside the listbox + debuff row. The +4px is taken from the listbox width via the parametric formula — no other geometry needs manual adjustment.

2. **logW formula becomes parametric on barW: `self.width - 20 - (barW + 18)`.** The `(barW + 18)` part means "bar width + 10 right margin + 8 gap between log and bar." Plan 04 hardcoded this as `self.width - 20 - 32` for barW=14 (32 = 14 + 10 + 8). The new form makes the relationship explicit. Justification: future barW tweaks (or sandbox-driven sizing) won't drift logW out of sync; the formula self-documents the layout invariant.

3. **logY = 62 (was 54).** +8px clearance below stage label. The stage label is drawn at y=30 with UIFont.Medium (line height ~16-18px, descenders extend ~2-4px below baseline → glyph bottom at ~y=48-50). Plan 04's logY=54 left only ~4-6px clearance which the user observed as a visible clip on the 'g' in 'Stage: Stable'. logY=62 leaves ~12-14px clearance, accommodating descenders + a clean visual gap.

4. **Header divider at `drawRect(10, 50, panelInteriorWidth, 1, 1, 0.4, 0.4, 0.4)` drawn in `:render` after the stage label, before refreshLogList.** Color matches the existing panel border `{r=0.4,g=0.4,b=0.4}` for consistency. Width is parametric: `self.width - 20 - (barW + 18)` so the divider stops at the listbox edge (does not extend under the bar column). Y=50 sits exactly between the stage label glyph bottom (~48) and the new logY (62), making the seam intentional.

5. **Listbox: `backgroundColor = {r=0.08, g=0.08, b=0.08, a=1}` AND `drawBorder = true`.** Two non-mutually-exclusive fixes for GAP-04. The opaque dark-grey bg makes the listbox interior distinct from the panel bg (panel is `{r=0,g=0,b=0,a=0.8}` — listbox now reads as slightly lighter and fully opaque). The `drawBorder = true` flag is a vanilla ISScrollingListBox property (verified at ISScrollingListBox.lua line 484: `if self.drawBorder then self:drawRectBorder(...) end`; default at line 716 is `drawBorder = false`). Setting it to true uses the listbox's own `borderColor` (vanilla default `{r=0.4,g=0.4,b=0.4,a=0.9}` at line 707) which already matches our visual style. NO manual `drawRectBorder` in `:render` is required — the listbox draws its own border in its own render pass.

6. **BAR_BORDER lightened from `(1, 0.4, 0.4, 0.4)` to `(1, 0.6, 0.6, 0.6)`** in the Pass 4 `drawRectBorder` call inside `:drawSanityBar`. Justification: the panel border (currently `{r=0.4,g=0.4,b=0.4,a=1}` per `o.borderColor` set in `:new`) is the same shade. With the bar pinned to the right edge of the panel, the bar's right border was visually fusing with the panel's right border. Lightening the bar border gives the bar a clear perimeter that separates it from the panel edge.

7. **CONTEXT.md is NOT mutated.** D-01..D-23 remain locked history-of-decisions. UI-SPEC.md is amended with provenance — same pattern Plan 04 used. Future readers can trace WHY the spec evolved by reading the Plan 05 amendment section.
